Flux = − coefficient × gradient

Think of holding a warm cup of coffee: heat flows from the hotter object to the colder hand. The same mathematical shape appears in Fourier’s law, Fick’s law, Ohm’s law, Darcy’s law, and more: the bigger the difference, the faster the change. Here we turn that into a human analogy: your state tends to move toward the people around you.
Rate of your change = − k × (you − environment)
